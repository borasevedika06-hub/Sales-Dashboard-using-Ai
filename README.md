# Sales-Dashboard-using-Ai 
LINK - https://borasevedika06-hub.github.io/Sales-Dashboard-using-Ai/

Problem Satement - The Core Problem Bajaj's CEO and CFO have no single place to look at business performance. The raw data sitting in Excel tells them nothing at a glance. 
Key Business Problems Hidden in the Data

1. Furniture is a trap — high revenue but Tables and Bookcases generate negative profit
2. Discount abuse — orders with 40%+ discounts are almost always loss-making
3. Geography blindspot — Central region underperforms vs West significantly
4. Customer illusion — some high-revenue customers are barely profitable
5. No seasonality awareness — Q4 is consistently the peak but not being capitalized on

The Solution Asked For
Build an interactive executive dashboard that:

1. Tells the story in 3 pages: Big Picture → Profit Leaks → Customer/Ops
2. Has 8 live filters so the CEO/CFO can slice any way they want
3. Surfaces smart insight callouts — no hunting, problems flagged automatically
4. Is boardroom-ready — clean, branded (navy + gold), printable as PDF

here are the samples of how the dashboard actually looks like there are various filters we can filter out so that we will get all the answers to the questions raised by the company

📊 Bajaj Executive Dashboard — Key Insights
🔢 Overall Business Health

1. Total Revenue: $2,835,729 across 9,994 transactions (2018–2021)
2. Total Profit: $286,397 at a blended margin of 10.1%
3. Average Order Value: $566 across 5,009 unique orders
4. 18.7% of all order lines lose money (1,871 rows) — burning $156K in hidden losses
5. Without loss-making orders, true margin potential is 15.6%

📅 Year-over-Year Trend
1. 2018 → 2019: Revenue dropped 27% ($778K → $565K) — biggest single-year dip
2. 2020: Revenue recovered to $757K (+34% rebound)
3. Margins improved every single year: 6% → 11% → 11% → 13%
4. 2021 hit the best-ever margin at 13% ($93K profit) despite flat revenue
5. Revenue has not recovered to 2018 peak levels — structural gap remains

📆 Seasonality
1. Q4 = 38% of annual revenue ($1.07M) and 39% of all profit ($110K)
2. November and December are the two highest-volume months every year without exception
3. Q1 is the weakest quarter — only 13% of annual revenue
4. January and February combined have fewer orders than a single strong month
5. September spikes every year due to corporate end-of-fiscal buying
6. No Q1 strategy exists — a major untapped revenue opportunity

🗺️ Regional Performance
1. West = top revenue region at $1.07M (38% share) but only 10% margin
2. East = most efficient region at 14% margin — highest profitability per dollar
3. South and Central both stuck at 8% margin — underperforming
4. Central Consumer is the worst region-segment combo at just 3.4% margin
5. East is the model region — its profitability structure should be replicated elsewhere

📦 Category Analysis
1. Furniture: $982K revenue (34.6% share) — but only 1.9% margin and $18K profit
2. Office Supplies: $971K revenue — 12.6% margin, $122K profit (6.6x more than Furniture)
3. Technology: $883K revenue — 16.5% margin, $145K profit (highest of all 3)
4. Furniture generates the most revenue but contributes almost nothing to profit
5. Technology is the smallest revenue category but the biggest profit contributor

🔍 Sub-Category Winners & Loser
Profit Champions:
1.Paper: 43.4% margin — $34K profit on $78K revenue
2. Envelopes: 40.0% margin
3. Copiers: 37.2% margin — generates 19% of all company profit alone
4. Fasteners: 31.3% margin
5. Accessories: 25.0% margin

Loss-Makers:
1. Tables: -5.8% margin — $303K revenue but -$17.7K net profit
2. Supplies: -2.5% margin
3. Bookcases: -2.2% margin
4. Combined, Tables + Bookcases + Supplies = -$22.4K total loss
5. Machines and Chairs are near break-even at 1.8% and 7.1%

👥 Customer Segment
1. Consumer: 58.3% of revenue but lowest margin at 8.1% — most over-invested
2. Corporate: 26.6% revenue share, 12.2% margin — stable and consistent
3. Home Office: smallest at 15.1% revenue share but best margin at 14.1%
4. If Consumer margin improved to 11% (matching Corporate) it would add ~$48K profit annually
[Corporate is the most stable segment — 11.8%–12.5% margin across all 4 regions]

1. East + Home Office = 21% margin — best combination in the entire business
2. Central + Consumer = 3.4% margin — worst combination, needs immediate intervention
[Corporate is the only segment that is profitable across all 4 regions]

🧑‍💼 Customer Analysis
1. Top revenue customer Brosina Hoffman: $303K revenue — only 0.3% margin ($803 profit)
2. Sean O'Donnell: $102K revenue — actually loss-making (-0.1% margin)
3. Claire Gute: $100K revenue — only 0.2% margin
[Top 3 revenue customers combined earn $972 total profit on $504K revenue (0.19% margin)]
1. Tamara Chand: $19K revenue — 47.1% margin, $8.9K profit (ideal customer profile)
2. Raymond Buch: $15K revenue — 46.1% margin (another ideal profile)
[Worst customers by loss: Cindy Stewart (-$6.6K), Grant Thornton (-$4.1K), Luke Foster (-$3.6K)]

📈 Category Growth 2018 → 2021
1. Furniture: -15.6% decline ($255K → $216K)
2. Office Supplies: -17.6% decline ($300K → $247K)
3. Technology: +21.6% growth ($223K → $272K) — only growing category
4. Furniture and Office Supplies together = 69% of revenue but both shrinking
[Technology is growing AND the most profitable — the clear strategic priority]
